Amoxicillin

Description

Amoxicillin is a broad-spectrum penicillin-class antibiotic used to treat a wide variety of bacterial infections.

Clinical Pharmacology

Amoxicillin inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding to penicillin-binding proteins, leading to cell lysis and death in susceptible organisms.

Clinical Studies

Clinical trials show amoxicillin is effective in treating infections of the respiratory tract, urinary tract, skin, and ear caused by susceptible bacteria.

Indication and Usage

Used for the treatment of otitis media, sinusitis, pneumonia, urinary tract infections, and Helicobacter pylori eradication (as part of combination therapy).

Contraindications

Contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to penicillins or beta-lactam antibiotics.

Warnings

Serious, occasionally fatal anaphylaxis has been reported. Discontinue immediately if allergic reaction occurs.

Precautions

Use with caution in patients with renal impairment. Prolonged use may result in fungal or bacterial superinfection.

Patient Counseling Information

Advise patients to complete the full course of therapy. May be taken with or without food. Diarrhea is a common side effect.

Drug Interactions

May interact with oral contraceptives, anticoagulants, and other antibiotics. Monitor for changes in INR or treatment efficacy.

Adverse Reactions and Side Effects

Common: nausea, vomiting, rash, and diarrhea. Serious: anaphylaxis, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, and C. difficile-associated diarrhea.

Drug Abuse and Dependence

Amoxicillin has no known potential for abuse or dependence.

Overdose

Overdose may cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and crystalluria. Provide symptomatic treatment and maintain hydration.
