Loratadine

Description

Loratadine is a non-sedating antihistamine used to treat allergies, including hay fever and hives.

Clinical Pharmacology

Loratadine selectively blocks peripheral histamine H1-receptors, reducing allergy symptoms without significant sedation.

Clinical Studies

Studies have demonstrated loratadine's effectiveness in reducing sneezing, nasal discharge, and itchy eyes associated with allergic rhinitis and urticaria.

Indication and Usage

Indicated for the relief of symptoms associated with seasonal allergic rhinitis and chronic idiopathic urticaria in adults and children.

Contraindications

Contraindicated in patients with hypersensitivity to loratadine or any component of the formulation.

Warnings

Use caution in patients with severe liver impairment. Accidental overdose may result in drowsiness and tachycardia.

Precautions

Although non-sedating, some patients may experience drowsiness. Evaluate alertness before operating heavy machinery.

Patient Counseling Information

Take once daily as directed. Inform healthcare providers of all medications being taken. Avoid alcohol if drowsiness occurs.

Drug Interactions

Minimal interactions. However, coadministration with erythromycin or ketoconazole may increase plasma concentrations.

Adverse Reactions and Side Effects

Common: headache, fatigue, dry mouth. Rare: tachycardia, liver function changes, allergic reaction.

Drug Abuse and Dependence

No evidence of abuse or dependence potential with loratadine.

Overdose

Symptoms may include drowsiness, rapid heartbeat, and headache. Provide supportive care and monitor vitals.

Dosage and Administration

Adults and children over 6: 10 mg once daily. For children under 6, dosage should be determined by a physician.

Medication Guide

Store at room temperature. Do not exceed recommended dose. Keep out of reach of children.
