Ibuprofen

Description

A nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used for pain relief, fever, and inflammation.

Clinical Pharmacology

Inhibits cyclooxygenase (COX-1 and COX-2) enzymes, reducing prostaglandin synthesis.

Clinical Studies

Effective in treating headaches, menstrual cramps, arthritis, and muscle pain.

Indication and Usage

Indicated for mild to moderate pain, inflammation, and fever reduction.

Contraindications

Allergy to NSAIDs, peptic ulcer disease, severe renal or hepatic impairment.

Warnings

Increased risk of GI bleeding and cardiovascular events with prolonged use.

Precautions

Use with caution in the elderly and in patients with cardiovascular conditions.

Patient Counselling Information

Take with food. Do not exceed the recommended dose. Monitor for stomach pain or bleeding.

Drug Interactions

May interact with aspirin, warfarin, SSRIs, and diuretics.

Adverse Reactions and Side Effects

Common: nausea, dizziness. Serious: ulcers, GI bleeding, heart attack.

Drug Abuse and Dependence

No known abuse potential.

Overdose

May cause nausea, vomiting, metabolic acidosis. Supportive care required.

Dosage and Administration

Typical adult dose: 200–400 mg every 4–6 hours as needed.

Medication Guide

Use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration. Stay hydrated.
