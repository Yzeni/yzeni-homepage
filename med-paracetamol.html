Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)

Description

Paracetamol, also known as acetaminophen, is a widely used analgesic and antipyretic agent.

Clinical Pharmacology

Paracetamol works centrally to inhibit prostaglandin synthesis, resulting in pain relief and fever reduction without significant anti-inflammatory effects.

Clinical Studies

Studies show paracetamol is effective for mild to moderate pain and fever, with rapid onset and low gastrointestinal risk compared to NSAIDs.

Indication and Usage

Used for the relief of fever, headaches, muscle aches, arthritis, backache, toothaches, colds, and menstrual cramps.

Contraindications

Contraindicated in individuals with severe liver disease or hypersensitivity to paracetamol or any component of the formulation.

Warnings

Risk of severe liver injury with high doses or chronic use. Avoid using more than the recommended dose or combining with other drugs containing acetaminophen.

Precautions

Use with caution in patients with liver disease or chronic alcohol use. Educate patients on recognizing symptoms of liver damage.

Patient Counseling Information

Inform patients to avoid exceeding 4,000 mg per day and to check all medications for acetaminophen content. Seek immediate care for signs of liver issues.

Drug Interactions

May interact with alcohol, warfarin (increased INR), and enzyme-inducing drugs. Monitor liver function with long-term or high-dose use.

Adverse Reactions and Side Effects

Generally well tolerated. Rare side effects include rash, hepatotoxicity, and allergic reactions such as swelling and difficulty breathing.

Drug Abuse and Dependence

Paracetamol does not cause dependence or abuse. However, intentional overdose for self-harm is a known risk.

Overdose

Overdose can result in severe, potentially fatal liver damage. Antidote: N-acetylcysteine is effective if given early. Seek emergency care immediately.

Medication Guide

Store in a dry place at room temperature. Do not use after expiration. Keep out of reach of children and avoid multiple paracetamol-containing products.
