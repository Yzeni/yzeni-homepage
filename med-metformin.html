Metformin

Description

An oral antidiabetic medication for type 2 diabetes mellitus.

Clinical Pharmacology

Decreases hepatic glucose production, decreases intestinal absorption of glucose, and improves insulin sensitivity.

Clinical Studies

Demonstrated significant reduction in HbA1c and fasting glucose levels.

Indication and Usage

Indicated for the management of type 2 diabetes in adults and children over 10.

Contraindications

Severe renal impairment, metabolic acidosis, or hypersensitivity.

Warnings

Risk of lactic acidosis; monitor renal function.

Precautions

Discontinue temporarily for contrast imaging procedures.

Patient Counselling Information

Take with meals to minimize GI upset. Monitor blood sugar regularly.

Drug Interactions

Cimetidine may increase metformin levels. Use caution with alcohol.

Adverse Reactions and Side Effects

Common: nausea, diarrhea. Rare: lactic acidosis.

Drug Abuse and Dependence

No abuse potential.

Overdose

Can cause lactic acidosis. Hospitalization may be required.

Dosage and Administration

Initial: 500 mg twice daily or 850 mg once daily. Max: 2,000 mg/day.

Medication Guide

Stay hydrated, avoid alcohol. Report symptoms like muscle pain, fatigue, or trouble breathing.
